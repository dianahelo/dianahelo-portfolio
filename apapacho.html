Overview
In the fast pace of the city, we all need a moment of pause. A breather. A snuggle. Apapacho is a collection of transformable textiles designed to wrap you in warmth, comfort, and love — bringing you back to that feeling of home, no matter where you are.
01
The Problem

This project was born from a universal experience: arriving home after an exhausting day in search of an emotional refuge. A gesture that envelops and comforts us. A hug — understood as an embrace for the soul, according to the words of those interviewed during the research phase.

The question was: what is it that physically embraces us? The most obvious, tangible, and warm answer is textiles. From that premise, three products were developed that literally hug you back.

02
The Process

The patterns were designed around the abstraction of an embrace — a curved "U" shape seeking to connect with another, evoking union and closeness. The color selection was carefully considered to convey tenderness and warmth, while bringing a touch of dynamism and fun.

The base module — a curved tubular shape — gave rise to all three patterns. Its scale and proportion were key: the module in the blanket is exactly half the height of one row in the scarf pattern, creating a visual language that flows across every object in the collection.

Pattern Narrative
Two elements.
They meet.
They embrace.
In the blanket — the main piece — a story is told. Two elements begin separately, each at one end. As they progress, they meet, intertwine, and finally embrace at the center, where the modules weave into a complex interaction that symbolizes the act of snuggling.
