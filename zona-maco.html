ZONA MACO
Overview
Commissioned to join Tec de Monterrey's merchandising team for their exhibition space at Zona MACO — Latin America's leading contemporary art fair. Designed patches for two of the four projects on display, creating wearable identities for cutting-edge research.
01
The Brief

Zona MACO brings together the most innovative galleries, artists, and institutions from across Latin America. Tec de Monterrey needed visual merchandising for their exhibition — specifically, patch designs that could represent complex research projects in a format that attendees could wear and take home.

The challenge was distilling academic research into visual symbols that felt contemporary, approachable, and worthy of one of the region's most prestigious art events.

02
The Process

Worked closely with the research teams to understand the core concepts behind each project. The first patch translates data visualization principles into a playful barcode aesthetic — colorful data points arranged in a grid that suggests both scientific rigor and artistic experimentation.

The second design takes a more direct approach, using binary code and data strings to create a typographic pattern that speaks to the intersection of technology and creativity that defines contemporary digital art research.

Design Philosophy
Art meets
data.
Each patch functions as a miniature data visualization — translating complex research into symbols that art fair attendees could instantly connect with. The designs bridge academic rigor with the playful, experimental spirit that defines contemporary art.
